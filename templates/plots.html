Dangers & Migration Routes

The region with most dead and/or missing migrants was the Mediterranean. This lends to the idea that it is the most dangerous area of migration, accounting for over 18,000 deaths. This could be due to having to primarily cross the sea with overcrowded boats, with limited access to food and water.

Overall, the most reliable data about migrants came from the route of Central America to the US, as most of the data had a source quality of 5. However, the Eastern Mediterranean also had strong source quality because their highest sq was also 5. The Western Mediterranean had slightly less reliable data, as the majority of their data points had a source quality of 3 or 4.

The majority of our data came from the Central America to US route, accounting for over half the data points. The next largest was the Central Mediterranean route. It is interesting to note that the Mediterranean was the most deadly route, but not the most frequently used route.

The causes of death show strong correlations to the regions of migration, as the Mediterranean accounts for most of the incidents of drowning and presumed drowning, North Africa accounts for most starvation or death due to sickness/ lack of medicines. It is interesting to note that the US-Mexico Border accounts for the majority of unknown + unknown(skeletal remains) causes of death.

About IOM

The IOM, or the International Organization for Migration is an organization that is focused on researching migration trends, try to ensure the health and safety of all migrants worldwide and are even involved with governments from around the world promoting policies that support migrant health and wellbeing. Especially during this difficult time where migrants are fleeing warzones from the Middle East or South American seeking a better life in the United States, the IOM is there to support these migrants to the best of their ability
